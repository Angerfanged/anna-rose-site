A Vision of Calm

This two-story stilted house design is a temporary haven for single families, as it provides stability and community during a tough transition that spans a couple of weeks to months. This residence is located in Hollywood Hills, California, which is conveniently close to Los Angeles where a lot of people travel in and out for work. The use of stilts is practical due to the changing topography in Hollywood Hills, but it also allows views of the city and nature surrounds the community's dwelling without harming the environment. Designed to blend in with the landscape, this community allows connection with other families who are experiencing the same situation.

This zone was framed with family-centered design and community in mind. It is a total of 18 homes, where each dwelling is a single-family residence with 2 bedrooms, 2 baths, an office, and open concept with kitchen, living and dining space. The interior is minimalistic, allowing families to add their personal touch as they come and go. It has many windows to provide views to the city of Hollywood hills, access to natural light, as well as a closeness to the nature that surrounds the neighborhood. The 2 large, curved windows allow for a perfect view within the master bedroom, and a perfect space to place the spiral staircase to maximize space. The spiral staircase that climbs through the entirety of the home provides a hierarchy that draws the eye while providing functional use.

The goal of this model is to provide not only a home, but a community for a traveling provider and their family to stay together.
