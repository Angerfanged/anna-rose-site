A Vision of Calm

The concept behind this design is sensory booths based on the six main colors of the light wave color spectrum: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and violet. Each color represents certain psychology and concepts that the human brain connects that color to. This sensory space is meant to be placed inside of or next to schools to provide a safe space for students to decompress throughout the school day. This notion allows grounding to enhance better productivity and overall better mental health.

The central layout creates a sense of unity and harmony to resonate with people on a deeper level. By using translucent colored vellum paper to allow tinted light to enter the space, it creates a sense of openness to compliment the theme of each colored room. The flat walls and arched doorways produce an inviting environment that the user would want to explore.

The senses that are explored in each booth are visual, auditory, olfactory (smell), and somatosensorial (touch). The use of these senses give an immersive multi-sensory experience that captures the user's attention.
